About Me

I'm a health and fitness journalist with 16 years' experience. Until I went freelance, I was Health Editor on Best magazine and Contributing Editor on Zest. After doing a Masters in journalism, I started out as a sub-editor – a great grounding for making me obsessive about checking facts and never, ever misquoting experts. Health and fitness has always been my passion and it's what I've written about since I became a journalist. I've also co-written a book on triathlon for beginners (Triathlon Made Easy).

I've written for...

Newspapers: The Guardian, The Observer, Daily Mail, Daily Express, The Independent

Magazines: Woman, Woman's Own, Good Housekeeping, Weight Watchers, Runner's World, Gurgle, Prima Baby, Women's Running, Top Santé, Triathlete's World, Best, Zest, AXA's Good Health, Lloyds Pharmacy, BUPA's Health News.

Websites: healthista.com, womenshealthmag.co.uk, londonist.com

I provide well-researched, engaging features and news pages for national newspapers, magazines and websites. I also produce sponsored content for corporate clients, such as Shredded Wheat and Sony, and content for websites.

As a runner, triathlete, snowboarder and climber, I practise what I preach and regularly review events and kit. I'm not a fan of marketing claims that don't deliver, so I never write about anything that's a waste of money or copy from press releases.

My bulging contacts book is full of experts from oncologists and dietitians to exercise physiologists and sports coaches, ensuring that my copy is always backed up with the latest and safest expertise.

I also cover travel, having been on press trips all over the world, from paragliding and snowmobiling in the French Alps to scuba diving in the Philippines.
